### 🧠 My Wiring (Summary)

It all started with an elementary school teacher who taught me **HTML** (`<marquee>` included).  
In middle and high school I mastered **Pascal, C, C++ and got certified in Cisco CCNA**.  
In college I fell in love with hardware architecture, compilers, and signal processing.  
The quest for complexity led me to a **Master's in Microelectronics** with a thesis on **Perceptual Hashing for Encrypted Voice Authentication**.

The pandemic and depression slowed me down. But in the middle of that pause I remembered something:  
Back in middle school they made us collect PET bottles and talk about the **2030 Agenda**. What felt like an obligation back then became a **conviction**.

Today I use that solid scientific foundation to build tools that generate **social, environmental, and health impact**.  
Because well-told data saves lives, protects forests, and improves public policy.

#### 🌱 The Seed (Elementary School)
It all started with a teacher who taught me **HTML**. I remember writing `index.html` in Notepad and watching colors appear, images moving, and those glorious `<marquee>` tags. That's when I knew I wanted to be a programmer.

#### 🌿 The Roots (Middle & High School)
I started to understand that programming was **logic**. My first languages: **Pascal, Visual Basic, Java, C, and C++**. I got certified in **Cisco CCNA Routing & Switching**. That's where I learned that information doesn't travel by magic—it travels through packets and invisible protocols. During that time they also made us **collect PET bottles, do community cleanups, and study the 2030 Agenda**. It felt like an obligation. I didn't know that years later it would become my compass.

#### 🌳 The Trunk (Computer Engineering)
I fell in love with the guts of the machine: Hardware Architecture, Boolean Logic, Compilers, Embedded Systems, Signal Processing. I confirmed that I didn't just want to be the bricklayer of code; I wanted to understand the physics of the cement.

#### 🌲 The High Branches (Master's in Microelectronics)
The search for complexity took me to grad school: Cryptography, DSP, Perceptual Hashing for Encrypted Voice. I learned to find patterns where there's only noise. And also: knowledge is useless if it stays in a paper nobody reads.

#### ⛈️ The Storm (Pandemic, Depression & ADHD)
My brain doesn't always work consistently. It freezes. A global pandemic hit, along with depression and the feeling that I hadn't made the most of my degree. *"I can't show what's in my head."*

#### 🌼 The Blooming (Now)
I remembered the PET bottles. The 2030 Agenda. My 13-year-old self cleaning the school sidewalk. It was no longer an obligation: it was responsibility and community. Today I'm building my portfolio from scratch—slowly, but moving forward. My foundation in signals isn't for perfect code: it's for building tools that help us **know what we're losing** (or what we can heal).

</details>
